Routine Dental Care

At-Home Dental Care:

  1. Brushing: Brush your teeth twice a day with fluoride toothpaste for at least two minutes.

  2. Flossing: Floss daily to remove plaque and food particles between teeth.

  3. Mouthwash: Use an antimicrobial or fluoride mouthwash to help reduce bacteria and strengthen enamel.

  4. Healthy Diet: Limit sugary foods and drinks, and eat a balanced diet rich in calcium and vitamins.

  5. Hydration: Drink plenty of water to maintain saliva production, which helps wash away food particles and bacteria.

Professional Dental Care

  1. Regular Dental Checkups: Visit your dentist every six months for a routine exam.

  2. Professional Cleaning (Scaling & Polishing): A dental hygienist will remove plaque, tartar, and stains from your teeth.

  3. X-Rays (If Needed): Dentists may take X-rays to detect cavities, bone loss, or other hidden issues.

  4. Fluoride Treatment: Some patients receive fluoride applications to strengthen enamel and prevent decay.

  5. Sealants (If Recommended): Dentists may apply sealants on molars to protect against cavities.

  6. Treatment of Any Issues: If cavities, gum disease, or other problems are found, the dentist will recommend treatment such as fillings, deep cleaning, or other necessary procedures.
